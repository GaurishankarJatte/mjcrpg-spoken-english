Day 9 – English Q&A & Vocabulary

Questions & Answers

  1. Q: What is a noun?
    A: A noun is a word that names a person, place, thing, or idea. Example: "Teacher", "City", "Book".
  2. Q: What is a verb?
    A: A verb expresses an action or a state. Example: "Run", "Speak", "Exist".
  3. Q: What is an adjective?
    A: An adjective describes a noun. Example: "Beautiful", "Tall", "Blue".
  4. Q: What is an adverb?
    A: An adverb describes a verb, adjective, or another adverb. Example: "Quickly", "Very", "Well".
  5. Q: Difference between "their", "there", "they're"?
    A: "Their" = possessive, "There" = place, "They're" = they are.
  6. Q: What is a preposition?
    A: A word showing relation between a noun/pronoun and other words. Example: "On", "In", "Under".
  7. Q: What is a conjunction?
    A: A word that joins words, phrases, or clauses. Example: "And", "But", "Or".
  8. Q: What is the difference between "affect" and "effect"?
    A: "Affect" = verb, to influence; "Effect" = noun, the result.
  9. Q: How to form the past tense of regular verbs?
    A: Add "-ed" to the base form. Example: "Play → Played".
  10. Q: Give an example of a phrasal verb.
    A: "Look after" = take care of. Example: "I will look after the dog."
