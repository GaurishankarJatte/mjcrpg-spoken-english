Prepositions – Examples

In: Used for enclosed spaces or time periods. Example: I live in Mumbai. / I was born in 2005.
On: Used for surfaces, days, or dates. Example: The book is on the table. / I will meet you on Monday.
At: Used for specific points, places, or times. Example: Meet me at the bus stop. / The class starts at 9 AM.
By: Indicates the agent or deadline. Example: The book was written by him. / Submit the report by Friday.
For: Shows purpose, duration, or recipient. Example: This gift is for you. / I worked for 5 hours.
With: Shows accompaniment or possession. Example: I went to the park with my friend. / He came with a bag.
About: Indicates a topic. Example: We talked about the project.
From: Shows origin or starting point. Example: I am from Pune. / He walked from home to school.
To: Indicates direction or recipient. Example: I am going to the market. / I gave the book to her.
Between: Used for two items or people. Example: The shop is between the bank and the school.
