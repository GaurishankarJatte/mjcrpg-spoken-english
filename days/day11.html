Articles – Rules & Examples

  1. A: Used before singular nouns starting with a consonant sound. Example: I saw a cat in the garden.
  2. An: Used before singular nouns starting with a vowel sound (a, e, i, o, u). Example: She ate an apple for breakfast.
  3. The: Used for specific nouns or nouns already mentioned. Example: The book on the table is mine.
  4. Use a when introducing something for the first time. Example: I bought a pen yesterday.
  5. Use the when the listener knows what you are talking about. Example: The pen I bought yesterday is blue.
  6. An is used before silent consonants like 'hour'. Example: He arrived an hour ago.
  7. Do not use an article before uncountable nouns when speaking generally. Example: I like water. (Not: I like the water)
  8. Use the for oceans, rivers, mountains, and unique places. Example: The Himalayas are beautiful.
  9. Use a/an for jobs or professions. Example: She is a teacher. He is an engineer.
  10. Use the before superlatives. Example: He is the best player in the team.
