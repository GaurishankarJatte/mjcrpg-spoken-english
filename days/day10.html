Day 10 – English Q&A & Vocabulary

Questions & Answers

  1. Q: What is the difference between "who" and "whom"?
    A: "Who" = subject, "Whom" = object. Example: Who called you? To whom did you speak?
  2. Q: What is a compound-complex sentence?
    A: A sentence with 2+ independent clauses and 1+ dependent clause. Example: I went home because it rained, and I took an umbrella.
  3. Q: Difference between "say" and "tell"?
    A: "Say" = speak words, "Tell" = give information. Example: He said hello. He told me a story.
  4. Q: What is a modal verb?
    A: A verb that expresses ability, possibility, permission, or obligation. Example: can, may, must.
  5. Q: Difference between "some" and "any"?
    A: Some = affirmative sentences, Any = negative/questions. Example: I have some apples. Do you have any apples?
  6. Q: What is the difference between "few" and "little"?
    A: Few = countable nouns, Little = uncountable nouns. Example: Few books, little water.
  7. Q: What is reported speech?
    A: Reporting what someone said. Example: Direct: He said, "I am happy." Reported: He said that he was happy.
  8. Q: Difference between "since" and "for"?
    A: Since = starting point, For = duration. Example: I have lived here since 2010. I have lived here for 10 years.
  9. Q: How to use "used to"?
    A: To describe past habits or states. Example: I used to play football as a child.
  10. Q: Give an example of a phrasal verb.
    A: "Give up" = stop trying. Example: He decided to give up smoking.
